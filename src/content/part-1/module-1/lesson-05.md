A cognitive bias is a predictable, systematic error in how we process information and make judgments. It is a predictable output of a fast, efficient brain in situations where its shortcuts misfire. Experts fall for cognitive biases. People who are aware of a particular bias still fall for it. Having more time, more information, or higher stakes does not make the errors go away. And biases do not discriminate: research finds them across intelligence levels, professional backgrounds, and levels of experience.

**Return to the opening.** The reaction you had in Lesson 1, whether you updated your opinion quickly, slowly, or not at all, reflected automatic processing at work. It was a pull in a direction before deliberate analysis. That pull is what this course helps you recognize.
