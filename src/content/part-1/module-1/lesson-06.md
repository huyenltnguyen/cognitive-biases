  - kind: multiple-choice
    mode: single
    question: "Scenario 1: A committee is reviewing proposals for a community project. They read the first two and decide one is 'clearly the best,' then stop reviewing the rest. What's most likely happening?"
    options:
      - id: bias-1
        label: Likely a bias
        explanation: 'Stopping after the first two without reviewing the rest means they never tested their early impression against the full evidence — a classic fast-mode shortcut where the first option shapes the evaluation of everything that follows.'
      - id: reasonable-1
        label: Likely a reasonable judgment
        explanation: 'This might seem reasonable, but deciding a winner before reviewing all candidates means the conclusion was reached without full information. The fast mode produced confidence before the slow mode had a chance to work.'
    correct_option_ids:
      - bias-1

  - kind: multiple-choice
    mode: single
    question: "Scenario 2: A volunteer arts group has invested months of time and some funds preparing an outdoor mural. When contractors discover structural issues that will substantially increase cost and delay, the organizers decide to proceed, noting how much work has already been done. What's most likely happening?"
    options:
      - id: bias-2
        label: Likely a bias
        explanation: 'Deciding to continue primarily because of prior time and money invested, rather than a fresh assessment of current costs and benefits, is indicative of the sunk‑cost fallacy.'
      - id: reasonable-2
        label: Likely a reasonable judgment
        explanation: 'Emphasizing past investment rather than re‑evaluating current costs and benefits is characteristic of the sunk‑cost fallacy.'
    correct_option_ids:
      - bias-2

  - kind: multiple-choice
    mode: single
    question: "Scenario 3: After hearing multiple news stories about a particular type of opportunistic crime in the past week, a person upgrades the locks on their home even though crime statistics in their area show no change. What's most likely happening?"
    options:
      - id: bias-3
        label: Likely a bias
        explanation: 'Vivid news reports make a risk feel larger than it actually is (the availability heuristic). If the decision relies mainly on recent stories instead of local crime data, that suggests a bias.'
      - id: reasonable-3
        label: Likely a reasonable judgment
        explanation: 'Upgrading locks is a reasonable precaution. But if the choice is prompted mainly by recent news rather than changes in local statistics, it likely reflects the availability bias.'
    correct_option_ids:
      - bias-3
