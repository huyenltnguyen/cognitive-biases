You now have four tools that work by adding a concrete step to your process rather than relying on good intentions:

- Look for the strongest case against your current view before deciding.
- Check what usually happens in comparable situations before reasoning from the specifics of yours.
- Ask whether you would start this today if you had no prior investment.
- Write down your position before hearing what others think.

The final part puts everything together. You will see a real case where several of the patterns from Part 2 operated at once, and use the tools from this part to analyze what went wrong and what could have been different.
