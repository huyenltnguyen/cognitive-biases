People who have just learned about anchoring still anchor. People who have studied confirmation bias still seek confirmation. Knowing the name of a bias doesn't do much to stop it.

What helps is building a specific step into your process — something you actually do, not something you hope to remember. "Look for the strongest counterargument before deciding" is a step you can put on a checklist. "Try to be more objective" is not.

All four tools in this module work the same way. They add a concrete action to the point in your process where your automatic thinking would otherwise take over. The change comes from the action, not from better intentions.
