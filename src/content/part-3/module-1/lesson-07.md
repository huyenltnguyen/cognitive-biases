Most people have had the experience of sticking with something mainly because they already put so much into it. A project, a degree, a relationship, a plan that stopped making sense a while ago. The time and money are gone either way, but stopping feels like throwing them away.

One way to cut through that feeling is asking yourself, "Knowing what I know now, would I choose to start this today?"

You are not pretending the past didn't happen. The skills you built, the things you learned, the relationships you formed along the way — those are yours regardless. What the question removes is the emotional weight of the spent cost, the feeling that stopping would mean it was all for nothing. What it leaves is a clearer view of whether the path ahead is one you would actually choose.

Sometimes the answer is yes. The past investment built something real, and continuing makes sense on its own merits. Sometimes the answer is clearly no, and the only thing keeping you going is the reluctance to walk away from what you already spent.
