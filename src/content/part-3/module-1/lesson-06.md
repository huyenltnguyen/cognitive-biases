exercise:
  kind: multiple-choice
  mode: single
  question: 'Which estimate correctly applies the outside view?'
  options:
    - id: two-hundred
      label: "200 members — the team's specific advantages justify confidence in the original estimate."
      explanation: 'This is inside-view thinking: the specific advantages feel compelling and override the base rate. But almost every team planning a new initiative believes they have specific advantages — that belief is built into the inside view.'
    - id: one-thirty
      label: "130 members — a moderate adjustment for the team's preparation and network."
      explanation: 'This acknowledges the base rate but adjusts too far toward the inside view. The base rate says fewer than 20% of comparable programs exceed 100 members. A 130-member estimate places this program in the top tier without strong evidence it belongs there.'
    - id: sixty-to-eighty
      label: "60–80 members — start from the base rate, then adjust upward modestly for the team's specific advantages."
      explanation: "Correct. The outside view starts from the reference class (40-80 members) and adjusts modestly upward for the team's genuine advantages. Strong networks and committed leadership are real, but they're common among teams who still fall within the base rate."
    - id: forty
      label: '40 members — take the base rate at face value with no adjustment.'
      explanation: "The outside view doesn't mean ignoring specific information entirely — it means starting from the base rate and adjusting conservatively. Some upward adjustment for the team's demonstrated advantages is appropriate."
  correct_option_ids:
    - sixty-to-eighty
