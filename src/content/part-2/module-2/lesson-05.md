exercise:
  kind: multiple-choice

  question: "A neighborhood association has been discussing whether to install security cameras on the main street. The conversation began two weeks after a well-publicized break-in at a local business, covered extensively on local news and social media. At the meeting, most members describe the neighborhood as 'increasingly unsafe' — despite local crime statistics showing a 12% decline in reported incidents over the same period last year. Which of the following best describes what's happening?"
  options:
    - id: rational
      label: The members are responding rationally to a real increase in crime that the statistics don't yet reflect.
      explanation: "The statistics show a decline, not a lag. If crime were actually increasing, we'd expect reports to reflect it. There's no evidence here of a real increase that statistics are missing."
    - id: availability
      label: The members are judging how common crime is by how vividly and recently they can recall an example.
      explanation: 'Correct. The break-in was vivid and heavily covered, making crime easy to recall and therefore feel more common. The feeling of increased danger is a product of availability, not a change in actual frequency.'
    - id: manipulation
      label: The members are being manipulated by local media into perceiving a problem that doesn't exist.
      explanation: "Media coverage does increase availability, but 'manipulation' implies intent. The availability heuristic operates automatically — the members aren't being deceived, they're experiencing a predictable cognitive pattern."
    - id: caution
      label: The members are applying appropriate caution after a real event, even if the statistics lag behind.
      explanation: "Taking precautions after an incident isn't automatically a bias. But when the stated justification is that the area is 'increasingly unsafe' — directly contradicted by the data — the judgment is driven by availability rather than the evidence."
  correct_option_ids:
    - availability
