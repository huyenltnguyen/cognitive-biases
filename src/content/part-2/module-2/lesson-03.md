exercise:
  - kind: multiple-choice
    mode: single
    question: "Estimate 1: A traveler who recently watched a documentary about airline disasters rates the probability of a serious incident on their upcoming flight as '1 in 100.' What best describes this estimate?"
    options:
      - id: inflated-1
        label: Inflated by the availability heuristic
        explanation: "Correct. The documentary made dramatic examples easy to recall, which inflated the perceived probability well beyond the statistical reality. The estimate reflects ease of recall, not actual frequency."
      - id: deflated-1
        label: Deflated by lack of available examples
        explanation: "Deflation happens when examples are hard to retrieve — making an event feel rarer than it is. Here the opposite is happening: a vivid recent example is inflating the estimate."
      - id: calibrated-1
        label: Likely calibrated
        explanation: "A 1-in-100 probability of a serious incident on a single flight is far above what safety data shows. This is an inflated estimate driven by a recently encountered vivid source."
    correct_option_ids:
      - inflated-1

  - kind: multiple-choice
    mode: single
    question: "Estimate 2: A college student, asked how deadly diabetes is compared to plane crashes, guesses they cause about the same number of deaths each year. What best describes this estimate?"
    options:
      - id: inflated-2
        label: Inflated by the availability heuristic
        explanation: "Inflation would mean overestimating something due to easy recall. Here the student is underestimating diabetes deaths, not overestimating them."
      - id: deflated-2
        label: Deflated by lack of available examples
        explanation: "Correct. Diabetes kills tens of thousands of Americans annually — far more than plane crashes. But diabetes deaths are slow and unremarkable, so they rarely appear in news coverage and leave little trace in memory. The estimate reflects how rarely these deaths come to mind, not how rarely they occur."
      - id: calibrated-2
        label: Likely calibrated
        explanation: "Diabetes causes far more deaths annually than commercial airline crashes. The student's estimate reflects the visibility of plane crashes in the news, not the actual frequency of either cause."
    correct_option_ids:
      - deflated-2

  - kind: multiple-choice
    mode: single
    question: "Estimate 3: An actuary at an insurance company, drawing on ten years of claims data across thousands of policies, estimates that roughly 1 in 5 male smokers over 60 will have a cardiac event within five years. What best describes this estimate?"
    options:
      - id: inflated-3
        label: Inflated by the availability heuristic
        explanation: "The actuary is not relying on ease of recall — they are working from large-scale statistical records. There is no reason to expect the availability heuristic is distorting this estimate."
      - id: deflated-3
        label: Deflated by lack of available examples
        explanation: "Deflation occurs when limited personal exposure makes an event feel rarer than it is. The actuary's estimate is grounded in representative data, not personal exposure."
      - id: calibrated-3
        label: Likely calibrated
        explanation: "Correct. The actuary is drawing on representative, large-scale data rather than on ease of recall. When estimates are based on systematic records rather than memory retrieval, the availability heuristic has little room to distort the result."
    correct_option_ids:
      - calibrated-3
