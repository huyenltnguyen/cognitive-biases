Before reading the explanation, look at the left column: three comparisons of lethal causes. The middle column shows what people estimated when asked which was more common.

When you're ready, reveal the third column to see what the data actually showed.

The question to hold: **why does the estimate column look the way it does?**

Source: Lichtenstein, S., Slovic, P., Fischhoff, B., Layman, M., & Combs, B. (1978). Judged frequency of lethal events. _Journal of Experimental Psychology: Human Learning and Memory_, 4(6), 551–578.

All figures are annual US death counts drawn from National Center for Health Statistics records, averaged over the early 1970s.
