title: What might be acting as an anchor?
part: 2
module: 1
lesson_number: 6
lesson_type: exercise
estimated_minutes: 3
gate_required: true
order_index: 14
prompt: >
  A product team meets to scope a new feature request. Before discussion opens,
  the manager mentions that a similar feature took about three months to build at
  a previous company. One team member describes it as "not that different from
  what we built last year." A stakeholder messages that they'd like it in the
  next quarter, and someone offhandedly recalls a past project that was delayed
  by an integration bug. After an hour of discussion, the team agrees on a
  ten-week timeline.
attempts_allowed: -1
exercise:
  kind: multiple-choice
  mode: multi
  question: 'Which of the following might have acted as an anchor in this conversation?'
  success_feedback: 'The manager''s numeric timeline and the "not that different" framing acted as anchors because they appeared early and shaped subsequent judgments.'
  options:
    - id: not-different
      label: The description of the work as "not that different from what we built last year."
    - id: ten-weeks
      label: The team's final agreement on ten weeks.
    - id: three-months
      label: The three-month timeline from the manager's previous company.
    - id: stakeholder-deadline
      label: A stakeholder messaging that they'd like it in the next quarter.
    - id: past-delay
      label: A colleague recalling a past project that was delayed by an integration bug.
  correct_option_ids:
    - three-months
    - not-different
