A school district is deciding whether to renew its contract with the vendor that runs their cafeteria services. The contract comes up for renewal every three years. This year, there have been two formal complaints about food quality and one about a billing error.

The person leading the review has worked with this vendor for six years and helped bring them on. She wants to do the review properly. For staff input, she reaches out to two colleagues who have been in their roles the longest and know the cafeteria operations well. Both speak highly of the vendor. The billing complaint comes with documentation, so she traces it step by step and finds a procedural error on the school's side — not the vendor's. The food quality complaints are harder to assess: they are vague, there are only two in three years, and she is not sure how much weight to give them. She notes them in her summary as "isolated incidents" and recommends renewal.

Before continuing, notice how the information was gathered and weighed in this review.
